Two ratios, one scale

The same type bends at every width

A fixed ratio keeps proportions identical everywhere. A dynamic ratio interpolates between two different scalars — compressing hierarchy on small screens for readability, expanding it on large screens for editorial impact. The typeface adapts to the medium.

WCAG consideration at step 5
The dynamic ratio produces steep growth at the upper end — step 5 can exceed the 200 % zoom threshold between ~1243 px and ~2268 px viewport width. This primarily affects the hero heading and can be mitigated with a separate font-size at extreme widths.
